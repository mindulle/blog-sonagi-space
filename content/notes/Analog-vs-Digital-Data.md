# Analog vs. Digital Data

Analog data is continuous information represented by physical quantities, like sound waves or temperature, that vary smoothly over time. Digital data, on the other hand, is discrete information represented by numerical values, typically binary digits (0s and 1s), allowing for precise storage and man
ipulation by computers.
