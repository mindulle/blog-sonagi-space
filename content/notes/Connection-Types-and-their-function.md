# Connection Types

Different devices connect to networks in various ways. **Ethernet** cables create a wired connection, often used for desktops and servers, providing a reliable and fast link. **Wi-Fi** offers wireless connectivity through radio waves, commonly found in laptops, smartphones, and IoT devices, allowing
 mobility within a network's range. **Bluetooth** is another wireless technology, primarily used for short-range connections between devices like headphones and smartphones. **Fiber-optic** connection
s utilize light to transmit data, offering very high bandwidth and are used for long-distance communication and backbone networks. **Cellular connections** use mobile networks to provide internet access to devices like smartphones and tablets, allowing connectivity virtually anywhere within cellular
 coverage.
