# Behavioral Economics

Behavioral Economics is a subfield of economics that studies the psychological, social, and emotional factors that influence decision-making and economic behavior. It seeks to understand why people make choices that deviate from the traditional economic model, which assumes that individuals behave r
ationally and seek to maximize their utility.
