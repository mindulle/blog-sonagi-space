# SSL and TLS Basics

Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) and Transport Layer Security (TLS) are cryptographic protocols that provide secure communication over a network. They work by encrypting data exchanged between a client and a server, ensuring confidentiality and integrity. SSL was the predecessor to TLS, and while SSL is n
ow considered outdated and insecure, the term "SSL" is often still used interchangeably with "TLS." The protocols establish a secure connection using digital certificates for authentication and encryp
tion algorithms for data protection.
