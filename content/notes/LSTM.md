# Long Short-Term Memory Networks (LSTMs)

LSTMs are a special kind of recurrent neural network (RNN) architecture designed to handle the vanishing gradient problem that often occurs when training standard RNNs. They excel at processing sequential data by maintaining a "memory" of past inputs, allowing them to learn long-term dependencies. T
his memory is controlled by gates that regulate the flow of information into and out of the cell state, enabling LSTMs to selectively remember or forget information over time.
