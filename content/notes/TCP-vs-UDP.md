# TCP vs UDP

TCP, or Transmission Control Protocol, is a connection-oriented protocol that guarantees delivery of packets to the destination router. It includes error-checking and ensures packets are delivered in the correct order. On the other hand, UDP, or User Datagram Protocol, is a connectionless protocol w
hich doesn't guarantee delivery or ensure correct sequencing of packets. As a result, UDP is considered faster and more efficient for some applications like video streaming or online gaming where spee
d matters more than accuracy. So, choosing between TCP and UDP really boils down to the specific requirements and priorities of the situation - it's a trade-off between speed and reliability.
